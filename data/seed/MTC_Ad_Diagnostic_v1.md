# MTC Facebook Ads — Diagnostic & Creative System (v1)

**Sample:** 46 ads with full creative + performance data, ~44,500 EGP spend, 2,394 messaging conversations started, late 2022 – April 2026.
**Blended cost per conversation:** 18.59 EGP. **Spread:** best ad 6.59 EGP, worst 80.06 EGP — a 12x range across ads in the same business, same audience, same period.

---

## 1. The five things the data is screaming at you

These are ranked by how much money they're costing you right now.

**1. Holiday-themed soft-sell ads cost ~2x what your normal ads cost.** Ramadan-greeting ads ("🌙 رمضان كريم ياباشمهندسين") and Eid-gift ads ("العيدية السنة") averaged 45–66 EGP per conversation. Your non-holiday ads averaged ~17–22 EGP. The greeting hijacks the first line — the slot that should be doing the selling. **Action:** stop running greeting-led ads. If you want to run a Ramadan campaign, lead with the offer; mention Ramadan in line 2 or in the visual.

**2. Specific-course ads beat all-courses ads.** The four ads that name a single specialty in the hook (decor, civil, civil 3D, architecture) blended at 11–20 EGP. The 31 ads that list "all engineering courses" blended at 20 EGP — and the worst of them blew up to 40+. **Action:** stop running "we teach everything" ads. Run one ad per course track, with that course's pain point in the hook.

**3. The "wanted: ambitious engineers" hook is your hidden gem.** "مطلوب مهندسين طموحين" produced 217 conversations at 13.64 EGP — 14.5 EGP blended across the two ads using this format. It mimics a job ad, which Egyptian engineers are conditioned to read carefully. You used it twice. **Action:** make this a permanent hook family. Run job-ad-style variants for every course track.

**4. Your hook is doing 90% of the work — and 90% of your hooks are wasted.** First 125 characters is what people see before "...See more." Winners use that real estate to deliver: who it's for + what's the offer + why now. Losers use it for "خصم حقيقي لكل المهندسين والطلبه في مصر" (vague greeting). **Action:** every new ad gets a hook that names the audience segment in the first 6 words.

**5. You're spending money on ad fatigue without knowing it.** The same Ramadan ad ran twice in different ad sets and one version was 33% more expensive than the other. Some ads got recycled across years (`EiD Fitr 22-4` was duplicated). Without a fatigue-watch process you're paying to show stale creative to people who've already seen it. **Action:** every ad gets a kill-switch — if cost-per-conversation rises 30% above its 7-day rolling average, pause it and refresh.

## 3. Hook templates that win (your swipe file)

These are the verbatim Arabic openers from your top performers. Copy these structures, swap the variables, and you have new ads that lean on what's already proven for your audience.

### Template A — Segment + Offer + Reality Check
> **Pattern:** `[لـ + audience segment]، [strong offer with anchor] [أقوى/حقيقي/مش هيتكرر]!`
> 
> **Example from your data (CPR 11.1):**
> "لمهندسي المدني (إنشاءات وطرق).. أقوى عرض بخصم 50% حقيقي!"
> 
> **Variables to swap:** segment (mechanical / electrical / interior / new graduates / 4th year students), offer detail (course pair, diploma, intensive), proof phrase (حقيقي / مش هيتكرر / لفترة محدودة).

### Template B — Job-Ad Mimicry
> **Pattern:** `مطلوب [target role description]! [setup question] [pivot to MTC as the solution]`
> 
> **Example from your data (CPR 13.6):**
> "مطلوب مهندسين طموحين! هل أنت مهندس أو طالب هندسة وعايز تطور مهاراتك في التصميم والهندسة؟"
> 
> **Why it works:** Egyptian engineering students scan job ads constantly. The hook borrows that attention.
> 
> **Variables to swap:** "مطلوب مهندس مدني خبرة BIM" / "مطلوب معماري يجيد Revit" / "مطلوب فني مكتب فني محترف" — match the course you're selling.

### Template C — Direct-Address Scarcity
> **Pattern:** `[audience, plural and respectful] يركزو في [العرض / الفرصة] ده [ما يميزه]`
> 
> **Example from your data (CPR 12.7):**
> "طلاب هندسة والسادة المهندسين يركزو في العرض ده مش هيتكرر.."
> 
> **Variables to swap:** segment, urgency reason ("قبل ما يخلص الخصم" / "قبل ما تقفل المجموعة" / "قبل بدء الكورس").

### Template D — Future-Vision + AI Modernization
> **Pattern:** `ابدأ مستقبلك في [field] مع MTC.. [modern angle: AI / BIM / 2026]`
> 
> **Example from your data (CPR 11.7, also 14.3):**
> "ابدأ مستقبلك في العمارة مع MTC.. بذكاء واحترافية!"
> "ابدأ مستقبلك في التصميم الداخلي باحتراف"
> 
> **Why it works:** the AI/2026 framing differentiates MTC from old-school training centers. This is your moat — lean in.

### Template E — Group / Social Hook
> **Pattern:** `جمع اصاحبك [verb describing benefit] [offer]`
> 
> **Example from your data (CPR 13.6):**
> "جمع اصاحبك واستفيد بخصومات بتوصل 50%"
> 
> **Why it works:** Egyptian engineering students enroll in friend groups; the group discount is real and the social proof is built into the offer mechanic.

## 4. Hook patterns to retire

| Pattern | Why it loses |
|---|---|
| `🌙 رمضان كريم ياباشمهندسين خلينا فاكريين شهر الخير...` | Holiday greeting steals the hook slot. Your audience scrolls past. |
| `حققنا المعادلة الصعبة...` | Abstract claim. *What* equation? Reader can't tell what's being sold in line 1. |
| `مش نقص شغل... نقص مهارة!` | Philosophical. Sounds like a Twitter quote, not a course offer. |
| `انك تحترف برامج اوتوديسك مبقاش اختيار` | Lecturing the customer. Egyptian audiences feel patronized by this register. |
| `Muttaheda Training Center is AUTODESK Authorized...` | English to Arabic-speaking buyers. |
| `العيدية السنة من مركز المتحدة بتوصل 50%` | "Eidiya" framing makes it feel like a gift, which obscures the price anchor. |
| Wall of text dumping all 6 specialties | When you list everything, no one feels addressed. |

## 6. The AI-driven creative loop

This is the workflow to replace the family-and-friends process. Treat it like a weekly cadence.

### Weekly rhythm

**Monday — Diagnose**
Export last 7 days of performance. Append rows to your `tagged.csv` library. Ask AI:
> *"Here's last week's ad performance. Which angles, hooks, and courses had the biggest cost-per-conversation increases vs the prior week? Which winners look fatigued (rising CPR, falling CTR)? Recommend 3 to pause."*

**Tuesday — Generate**
Pick 2 angles to push this week (one from your proven top-4, one experimental). Ask AI:
> *"Generate 5 hook variants for the [angle name] template, selling [course], targeting [segment]. Each hook must (1) name the audience in the first 6 words, (2) state the offer concretely with discount %, (3) include one reason-to-act-now. Output Arabic with Egyptian dialect (3amiya), match the register of these proven examples: [paste 2-3 winning hooks]."*

**Wednesday — Build**
Pair each hook with a visual brief (image or 15-second vertical video). For video, ask AI:
> *"Write a 15-second vertical video script for [hook]. Format: 0-3s hook (on-screen text + voiceover), 3-10s benefit (3 specific bullets), 10-15s CTA (specific deadline + WhatsApp). Egyptian Arabic, voice register: [match from winners]."*

**Thursday — Launch**
Publish 4–6 ad variants in a single ad set, Messages objective, 50–100 EGP/variant/day. Name them with this convention:
`[date]_[course]_[angle]_[variant#]`
e.g. `2026-05-12_decor_jobad_v2` — so the data is self-documenting.

**Friday → next Monday — Watch and kill**
Per variant, kill condition: spent ≥80 EGP AND cost-per-conversation > 30 EGP, OR has been live 5 days. Scale winners by duplicating the ad set with 2x budget rather than editing the original (preserves the algorithm's learning).

### Monthly rhythm

Once a month, ask AI:
> *"Here's all data from the last 30 days, tagged. (1) Which new angles emerged that we should formalize into the angle bank? (2) Which previous winners are showing fatigue across all variants and need full creative refreshes? (3) What audience segments show different angle preferences (e.g., 4th-year students vs new grads vs working engineers)?"*

## 7. What this analysis cannot tell you (yet)

Being honest about the limits so you don't over-trust the conclusions:

1. **No conversion-to-paid data.** Cheap conversations ≠ cheap students. Some of your best CPR ads might be attracting time-wasters who never enroll. Fix: in your CRM (or even a Google Sheet), log every Messenger conversation's source ad name and final outcome (enrolled / quoted / ghosted). After 60 days you'll know which CPR-cheap ads are also enrollment-cheap.

2. **Older ads (2018–2022) excluded.** Your performance file only covers post-April 2023. The first three years of ads (~315 of them) might contain different lessons.

3. **n=1 on several angles.** "Future-vision + AI" looks great but it's one ad with 23 EGP spent. Test it 3–5 more times before you fully trust the number.

4. **Recency confound.** Recent ads (2025–2026) are both better-written *and* use better angles. We can't fully separate "modern copy is winning" from "specific angles are winning."

5. **No video data in this sample.** You only had 1 video ad in the entire creative export. The fact that video isn't in your performance picture *at all* is itself a finding — it's the biggest format you're not using.

6. **No audience-segment performance breakdown.** Whether students-only audiences outperform graduates-only audiences requires re-exporting Ads Manager with audience-breakdown columns.
