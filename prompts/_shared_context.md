## Hook Templates A–E (§3 — proven Arabic openers, verbatim)

### Template A — Segment + Offer + Reality Check
**Pattern:** `[لـ + audience segment]، [strong offer with anchor] [أقوى/حقيقي/مش هيتكرر]!`

**Proven example (CPR 11.1 EGP):**
> "لمهندسي المدني (إنشاءات وطرق).. أقوى عرض بخصم 50% حقيقي!"

**Variables:** segment (mechanical / electrical / interior / new graduates / 4th-year students), offer detail (course pair, diploma, intensive), proof phrase (حقيقي / مش هيتكرر / لفترة محدودة).

### Template B — Job-Ad Mimicry
**Pattern:** `مطلوب [target role]! [setup question] [pivot to MTC as solution]`

**Proven example (CPR 13.6 EGP — best in dataset):**
> "مطلوب مهندسين طموحين! هل أنت مهندس أو طالب هندسة وعايز تطور مهاراتك في التصميم والهندسة؟"

**Why it works:** Egyptian engineering students scan job ads constantly. The hook borrows that attention.

**Variables:** "مطلوب مهندس مدني خبرة BIM" / "مطلوب معماري يجيد Revit" / "مطلوب فني مكتب فني محترف"

### Template C — Direct-Address Scarcity
**Pattern:** `[audience plural] يركزو في [العرض/الفرصة] ده [ما يميزه]`

**Proven example (CPR 12.7 EGP):**
> "طلاب هندسة والسادة المهندسين يركزو في العرض ده مش هيتكرر.."

**Variables:** segment, urgency reason (قبل ما يخلص الخصم / قبل ما تقفل المجموعة / قبل بدء الكورس).

### Template D — Future-Vision + AI Modernization
**Pattern:** `ابدأ مستقبلك في [field] مع MTC.. [modern angle: AI / BIM / 2026]`

**Proven examples (CPR 11.7 and 14.3 EGP):**
> "ابدأ مستقبلك في العمارة مع MTC.. بذكاء واحترافية!"
> "ابدأ مستقبلك في التصميم الداخلي باحتراف"

**Why it works:** AI/BIM framing differentiates MTC from old-school training centers.

### Template E — Group / Social Hook
**Pattern:** `جمع اصاحبك [verb describing benefit] [offer]`

**Proven example (CPR 13.6 EGP):**
> "جمع اصاحبك واستفيد بخصومات بتوصل 50%"

**Why it works:** Egyptian engineering students enroll in friend groups; group discount is real and social proof is built into the offer.

## Retire List (§4 — patterns that consistently lose)

| Pattern | Why it fails |
|---------|-------------|
| `🌙 رمضان كريم ياباشمهندسين...` | Holiday greeting steals hook slot (CPR 56.1) |
| `حققنا المعادلة الصعبة...` | Abstract — reader can't tell what's being sold in line 1 |
| `مش نقص شغل... نقص مهارة!` | Philosophical — sounds like Twitter, not a course offer (CPR 66.1) |
| `انك تحترف برامج اوتوديسك مبقاش اختيار` | Lecturing — audience feels patronized |
| `Muttaheda Training Center is AUTODESK Authorized...` | English to Arabic-speaking buyers |
| `العيدية السنة من مركز المتحدة بتوصل 50%` | "Eidiya" framing obscures price anchor |
| Wall of text dumping all 6 specialties | When you list everything, no one feels addressed |

**Core rule (§1):** Every hook must name the audience segment in the **first 6 words**. Winners: who it's for + what's the offer + why now. Losers: vague openers.

| Civil 3D / Surveying | First-time-in-Egypt | "لاول مرة في مصر.. هتفهم السيفيل ثري دي..." |

**§5 rule:** Don't run more than 1–2 cross-track ads at a time — they crowd out specific-course ads.

## Kill / Scale Rules (§6)

**Kill:** spent ≥80 EGP AND CPR >30 EGP, OR live ≥5 days → pause the ad
**Fatigue:** CPR rises 30% above 7-day rolling average → pause + refresh creative
**Scale:** CPR ≤17 EGP after ≥80 EGP spend → duplicate ad set at 2× budget (never edit the original — preserves algorithm learning)

**Naming convention:** `[date]_[course]_[angle]_[variant#]` — e.g. `2026-05-12_decor_jobad_v2`

---

## Data Limitations to Acknowledge (§7)

- No conversion-to-paid data: cheap CPR ≠ cheap students. Log Messenger → enrollment outcomes.
- n=1 on several angles (Future-vision + AI had only 1 ad, 23 EGP spend) — directional only.
- No video data in sample — video is the biggest untapped format.
- No audience-segment performance breakdown — demographic preferences are inferred, not measured.
